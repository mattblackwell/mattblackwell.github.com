Hello, I am a sixth-year PhD candidate in the Department of Government at Harvard University. I am also an associate of the Institute for Quantitative Social Science. For two years, I was the coordinator for the Applied Statistics Workshop at IQSS. I am also a contributor to the Social Science Statistics blog on occasion.

My research focuses on American politics and political methodology. In American politics, I study campaigns, the effectiveness of negative advertising, and Congressional staff networks. In methods, I work on dynamic causal inference, missing data, panel data, and social network analysis.
